Remainders that refuse to change

Find all positive integers \(n\) such that \(n^2+3n+2\) is divisible by \(n+4\).

Rewrite the expression in terms of \(n+4\). Equivalently, compute the remainder when \(n^2+3n+2\) is divided by \(n+4\).

2

A triangle with a hidden symmetry

In triangle \(ABC\), suppose \(AB=AC\). Points \(D\) and \(E\) lie on \(AB\) and \(AC\), respectively, such that \(BD=CE\). Prove that \(BE=CD\).

Try comparing triangles \(\triangle BEC\) and \(\triangle CDB\). Notice that \(AB=AC\) and \(BD=CE\) imply \(AD=AE\).

3

Odd sums on a small board

How many ordered pairs \((a,b)\) with \(1\leq a,b\leq 20\) have \(a+b\) odd and \(ab\) divisible by \(3\)?

Separate the numbers from \(1\) to \(20\) by parity and by divisibility by \(3\). The condition \(a+b\) odd means one number is odd and the other is even.
