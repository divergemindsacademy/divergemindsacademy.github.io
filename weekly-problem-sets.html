Sample Set: Week 1

Attempt these in order. Write complete arguments, not just final answers. The hint buttons reveal small nudges only.

AlgebraMedium
1

A symmetric expression

Let \(x,y,z>0\) and \(xyz=1\). Prove that \[\frac{x}{x^2+x+1}+\frac{y}{y^2+y+1}+\frac{z}{z^2+z+1}\leq 1.\]

Try substituting \(x=\frac{a}{b}\), \(y=\frac{b}{c}\), \(z=\frac{c}{a}\). Then look for a common denominator or a known inequality.

Number TheoryMedium
2

Consecutive divisibility

Prove that for every integer \(n\), the number \(n^5-n\) is divisible by \(30\).

It is enough to prove divisibility by \(2\), \(3\), and \(5\). For \(5\), consider residues modulo \(5\).

GeometryProof
3

Equal chords, equal insight

In a circle, points \(A,B,C,D\) lie in this order and \(AB=CD\). Prove that \(\angle ACB=\angle CAD\).

Equal chords subtend equal angles in the same circle. Identify the chord seen by each angle.

CombinatoricsChallenge
4

Coloring a row

A row of \(10\) squares is colored red, blue, or green. How many colorings have no two adjacent squares of the same color and use all three colors at least once?

First count all colorings with no equal adjacent colors. Then subtract colorings that use only two colors.
